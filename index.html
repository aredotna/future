Plans + Fundraising, August 2014
Arena is a platform for private and collaborative research where you can save links, images, and files as "blocks" and organize them into named "channels." People use Arena to assemble references around a topic, collect images around a theme, share ideas with collaborators, and more.

We built Arena in response to feeling overwhelmed by the mass of information presented to us daily on the Internet. We wanted a tool that could help us collect and sort information as it appears. Arena enables you to build ideas over time using any content you come across in the world. We like to think of Arena as an evolving archive where people collaboratively create new concepts by combining new and existing material they find online and elsewhere.

Over the past two years, the platform has hosted a vibrant, closed community of artists, designers, writers, educators, and researchers. We've studied the dynamics, gathered feedback from our users, and now we're ready to make Arena available to the public.





We are currently redesigning the entire platform, streamlining around its core features. Toward the end of the year, we will introduce an unobtrusive revenue model enabling Arena to be a sustainable and lasting business.

In order to help us get there, we are raising a small round of funding in the form of rewarded donations and equity investments. We've set our goal at $30,000, but we will accept any raised amount. We are committed to the project in any scenario.

Principles
• We aim to build a flexible platform that helps people collect, discover, contextualize, and share information, publicly and privately.

• We don't dictate how people can or should use Arena. The platform is designed to support arbitrary applications.

• We want Arena to persist for as long as people want to use it. We're committed to building a sustainable business and retaining complete control of the platform.

• We take privacy seriously, as well as anonymity for those who want it. We will never share user data without explicit consent and we support users who want to keep their online persona separate from their personal life.

• We believe that advertisements are a conflict of interest. Arena will never show ads.
